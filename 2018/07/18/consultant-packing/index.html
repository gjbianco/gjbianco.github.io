Guy's Packing Guide

Guy Bianco IV
2018-07-18

Background

I end up traveling a lot for work. That includes planes, trains, and even automobiles (which also happens to be one of my favorite movies). For the past couple years, I’ve been increasingly focused on reducing my luggage.

For the past year or so, I’ve been going entire work weeks with just a 28L bag. Recently, I’ve gotten down to carrying just a single 18L bag (Timbuk2 Lightweight Launch). For those that aren’t familiar with that size, imagine about the size of your laptop bag.

Even if you don’t have such an extreme goal, I will provide techniques for traveling lighter and give you the mindset to reduce what you’re carrying.

I realize this is not a new concept. There has been an “ultralight” movement for some years now. However, I don’t often see those ideas applied to business travel.

Motivation

There’s a sort of Zen to packing light. Sometimes, it’s all about the little things. It was startling to learn how big of a difference it makes to not have to worry about checked bags and overhead bins. If there is any way that you can carry just a single bag that fits under the seat in front of you on a plane, I highly recommend trying it.

For example, boarding groups no longer matter to you. In fact, it’s better to be one of the last people on the plane since that means less time sitting on the plane. The only reason you really want to get on the plane first is to fight for overhead bins. Also, you can’t have a lost checked bag if you don’t have one in the first place.

Toiletries

This is the section that will probably vary the most from person to person. Everybody has different products that they need to take with them. You should try to pack lighter, but the whole goal is to be more comfortable.

The biggest thing to keep in mind is to pack as much as you need. No more, no less. If you’re going for only a few days, you probably don’t need the giant vat of shampoo from Costco. Put a few days worth in a smaller container. Also think about things that you can get while you’re there. For example, a lot of hotels provide shampoo and conditioner. Also, unless you’re going to the woods somewhere, you can probably stop by a convenience store for some things.

You can get reusable containers of varying sizes from plenty of convenience stores. Keep in mind that they don’t have to be specifically for containing toiletries. Recently, I’ve been keeping a lot of liquid toiletries in small plastic Play-Doh containers (not yet sure how TSA is going to respond to them). I’ve also been using a pencil case as a small toiletry bag.

Clothes/Laundry

This is probably the area that has the biggest effect on your luggage volume. Clothes take up a lot of space and you probably can’t just pack one outfit (for various reasons). That being said, I’m convinced that most people carry way more than they really need. If you’re going on a 4 day business trip, you don’t need 5 days worth of clothing. You probably don’t even need 3 days worth of clothing.

The biggest difference for me was realizing that I can easily do laundry in my hotel room. You can buy little packs of detergent sheets for a couple bucks and each pack comes with dozens of sheets. To wash clothing, you really just need water, detergent, and something to agitate the clothing. You’ve got the detergent, a sink/bath, and your hands. Scrub the clothes and let them soak for a little bit, then you can hang dry them with a travel clothesline.

Usually, I only pack enough clothes for 2 or 3 days. This gives me enough buffer time to wash clothes and have them dried by the time I want to wear them (and enough where I can do laundry every other day instead of every day). Packing them in a compression sack makes it even smaller.

Specifically, for dress shirts, I recently found some non-iron button down shirts from Joseph A. Bank. I got them on clearance and you can just wash them without ironing. You can iron a little on warm or even use some water and a hairdryer to smooth out light wrinkles.
